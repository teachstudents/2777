☀️ Sun Protection 🕶️

  • 🧴 Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen every 2 hours
  • 🕶️ Wear UV-protective sunglasses
  • 👒 Use wide-brimmed hats
  • 🌳 Seek shade during 10am-4pm
  • 👕 Wear UV-protective clothing
🌊

🌊 Water Safety 🏊‍♀️

  • 👥 Never swim alone - buddy system
  • 🚩 Check water conditions & flags
  • 🏊‍♂️ Stay within designated swim areas
  • 💪 Know your swimming limits
  • ⚡ Watch for rip currents
🌡️

🌡️ Heat Prevention 💧

  • 💧 Drink water every 15-20 minutes
  • 🌳 Take breaks in shade/AC
  • 🚨 Recognize heat exhaustion signs
  • 🚫 Avoid alcohol and caffeine
  • 👕 Wear light-colored, loose clothing
⚠️

⚠️ Beach Hazards 🦀

  • 🪼 Watch for jellyfish and sea creatures
  • 🐚 Avoid sharp shells and rocks
  • 🔥 Be careful of hot sand
  • 📢 Check for beach warnings
  • 🌩️ Stay away from piers during storms
🚨

🚨 Emergency Preparedness 📞

  • 🏊‍♂️ Know location of lifeguards
  • 📞 Have emergency contacts ready
  • 📱 Carry a charged phone
  • 🩹 Know basic first aid
  • 📍 Plan meeting points with friends
🎒

🎒 Beach Essentials 🏖️

  • 💼 Waterproof bag for valuables
  • 🩹 First aid kit
  • 💧 Plenty of water and snacks
  • ☂️ Beach umbrella or tent
  • 🏖️ Towels and change of clothes

🗣️ Common Questions & Tips 📝

👤 "What's your surname and given name?"

🗣️ Speak clearly and slowly. Example: "My surname is Wang, and my given name is Xiaoming."

🔤 "How do you spell your first name?"

📝 Spell letter by letter. Practice phonetic alphabet if needed.

🌍 "Do you have an English name?"

✨ If yes, share it. If no, say "I prefer to use my Chinese name."

🎂 "When is your birthday?"

📅 Format: "My birthday is [Month] [Day], [Year]" or just month and day.

🏖️ Beach Activities & Safety 🛡️

🏖️ "What do you like to do at the beach?"

🏊‍♀️ Examples: swimming, volleyball, building sandcastles, reading, photography

🛡️ "What are your top safety tips?"

☀️ Pick 2-3 key points: sunscreen, swimming with buddy, staying hydrated

💡 Interview Tips 🎯

  • 👁️ Make eye contact with the reporter
  • 🗣️ Speak slowly and clearly
  • 😊 Smile and stay relaxed
  • ⏸️ It's okay to pause and think
  • 📚 Practice your answers beforehand
