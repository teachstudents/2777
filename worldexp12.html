Banksy: The Anonymous Artist

Vocabulary

  1. anonymous /əˈnɒnɪməs/: The artist chooses to remain anonymous, which adds to his mystery.
  2. graffiti /ɡrəˈfiːti/: His graffiti art often appears unexpectedly on public walls.
  3. stencil /ˈstensl/: He uses stencils to create his detailed images quickly.
  4. satirical /səˈtɪrɪkl/: His work is often satirical, using humor to criticize politics and society.
  5. social commentary /ˈsəʊʃl ˈkɒməntri/: His art provides sharp social commentary on issues like war and poverty.
  6. elusive /ɪˈluːsɪv/: Despite his fame, Banksy remains an elusive figure.
  7. provocative /prəˈvɒkətɪv/: His art is provocative, designed to make people think and react.
  8. subversive /səbˈvɜːsɪv/: The themes in his art are often subversive, challenging authority.
  9. installation /ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃn/: He created a famous art installation called "Dismaland."
  10. guerrilla art /ɡəˈrɪlə ɑːt/: This form of guerrilla art appears in public spaces without permission.
  11. identity /aɪˈdentəti/: His true identity is a subject of constant speculation.
  12. auction /ˈɔːkʃn/: One of his paintings famously self-destructed after being sold at auction.
  13. activism /ˈæktɪvɪzəm/: His work is a form of artistic activism.
  14. controversial /ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːʃl/: His methods are sometimes controversial, but his message is powerful.
  15. ephemeral /ɪˈfemərəl/: Graffiti art is often ephemeral, as it can be removed or painted over.
  16. iconic /aɪˈkɒnɪk/: "Girl with Balloon" has become one of his most iconic images.
  17. critique /krɪˈtiːk/: His work offers a sharp critique of consumerism.
  18. mainstream /ˈmeɪnstriːm/: He has successfully entered the mainstream art world on his own terms.
  19. authenticity /ˌɔːθenˈtɪsəti/: A special company authenticates his work to prevent fakes.
  20. impact /ˈɪmpækt/: The global impact of his art is undeniable.

Warm-up Questions

  1. Banksy uses art to comment on social issues. In China, what forms of art or media (like songs, movies, or online comics) do people use to express their opinions on society?
  2. Banksy is famous but anonymous. In an age of social media where everyone wants to be known, why do you think someone would choose to be anonymous? What are the advantages?
  3. His art is technically illegal graffiti, but it sells for millions. What do you think is the difference between art and vandalism? Where is the line?
  4. One of his artworks, "Girl with Balloon," represents hope. If you were to create a piece of art to represent the spirit of today's Chinese youth, what would it look like?
  5. Banksy's art often appears as a surprise. How would you react if you woke up one morning and found a world-famous artwork on a wall near your home?

Reading Passage

Banksy is a famous but anonymous artist. No one knows his real identity. He uses a stencil to make graffiti art on public walls. His pictures are simple but have a strong message. His art has a big impact on people who see it around the world.

Known for his provocative and often satirical art, Banksy provides sharp social commentary on important topics. His style of guerrilla art appears unexpectedly, and because it can be removed, it is often ephemeral. Although his work started on the street, he has successfully entered the mainstream art world. Consequently, his pieces sell for millions at auction, which requires a special company to check the authenticity of each one.

Ultimately, the cultural significance of Banksy's work lies in its role as a form of artistic activism. His subversive and frequently controversial critique of society challenges authority and consumerism. Creating iconic images like "Girl with Balloon" and large-scale installations such as "Dismaland," he has cemented his status as an elusive yet globally recognized figure, proving that art can be a powerful and transformative tool for change.

Barrington Irving: The Trailblazing Pilot

Vocabulary

  1. aviator /ˈeɪvieɪtə(r)/: He is a celebrated aviator known for his record-breaking flights.
  2. solo /ˈsəʊləʊ/: In 2007, he completed a solo flight around the world.
  3. circumnavigate /ˌsɜːkəmˈnævɪɡeɪt/: He was the youngest person at the time to circumnavigate the globe alone.
  4. aeronautics /ˌeərəˈnɔːtɪks/: He promotes education in science, technology, engineering, mathematics, and aeronautics.
  5. inspiration /ˌɪnspəˈreɪʃn/: His journey is an inspiration to millions of young people.
  6. non-profit /ˌnɒnˈprɒfɪt/: He founded a non-profit organization called Experience Aviation.
  7. mentor /ˈmentɔː(r)/: He had a mentor who was a professional pilot who encouraged him.
  8. scholarship /ˈskɒləʃɪp/: He turned down several football scholarships to pursue his dream of flying.
  9. logistics /ləˈdʒɪstɪks/: The logistics of his global flight required years of planning.
  10. expedition /ˌekspəˈdɪʃn/: He has led educational expeditions to remote locations around the world.
  11. STEM /stem/: His work focuses on encouraging students to pursue careers in STEM fields.
  12. record-breaking /ˈrekɔːd breɪkɪŋ/: His record-breaking flight took 97 days to complete.
  13. assemble /əˈsembl/: The plane he flew was assembled from donated parts.
  14. adversity /ədˈvɜːsəti/: He overcame significant financial adversity to achieve his goal.
  15. pioneer /ˌpaɪəˈnɪə(r)/: He is a pioneer in aviation education.
  16. ambition /æmˈbɪʃn/: His ambition was fueled by a chance encounter with a pilot.
  17. empower /ɪmˈpaʊə(r)/: His mission is to empower young people to follow their dreams.
  18. transformative /trænsˈfɔːmətɪv/: The experience of flying solo around the world was transformative for him.
  19. innovative /ɪnˈnɒvətɪv/: He uses innovative technology to bring his expeditions into classrooms.
  20. legacy /ˈleɡəsi/: His legacy is not just his flight, but the students he inspires.

Warm-up Questions

  1. Barrington Irving was inspired by a pilot he met. Think about your own career goals. Has a specific person or a chance event inspired you to pursue a certain path, like the way a great teacher might inspire you to study their subject?
  2. He turned down sports scholarships to follow his passion for flying. Have you ever had to choose between a "safe" path and a path you were truly passionate about? What are the risks and rewards?
  3. His organization, Experience Aviation, focuses on STEM. In China, there is a strong focus on science and technology. Why is this kind of education so important for a country's future?
  4. His plane was built from donated parts. This shows the power of community. Describe a time when you saw people in your community in China come together to help someone achieve a big goal.
  5. If you could take an educational expedition anywhere in the world, where would you go and what would you want to learn?

Reading Passage

Barrington Irving is a famous aviator. His big ambition was to fly around the world solo. His story is an inspiration to many young people. He is a true pioneer in flying and education.

In order to achieve his goal, Irving had to overcome significant financial adversity. He persuaded companies to donate parts, which allowed him to assemble his own plane. His record-breaking flight to circumnavigate the globe was a huge success, and this transformative experience led him to create a non-profit organization to help students.

After his flight, Irving, who had once turned down a sports scholarship to follow his passion, founded Experience Aviation to empower youth through aeronautics and STEM education. Following the path his mentor inspired, he now leads educational expeditions that involve complex logistics and use innovative technology. Ultimately, his lasting legacy is not just his historic flight, but his profound dedication to inspiring the next generation of leaders.

Amelia Earhart: The Pioneer of the Skies

Vocabulary

  1. pioneer /ˌpaɪəˈnɪə(r)/: She was a true pioneer of aviation, breaking barriers for women.
  2. aviation /ˌeɪviˈeɪʃn/: Her achievements had a lasting impact on the field of aviation.
  3. transatlantic /ˌtrænzətˈlæntɪk/: She was the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.
  4. navigator /ˈnævɪɡeɪtə(r)/: Her trusted navigator, Fred Noonan, accompanied her on her final flight.
  5. endurance /ɪnˈdjʊərəns/: Her long-distance flights required incredible skill and endurance.
  6. icon /aɪkɒn/: She became an international icon of adventure and female independence.
  7. disappearance /ˌdɪsəˈpɪərəns/: Her disappearance over the Pacific Ocean remains one of history's greatest mysteries.
  8. logbook /ˈlɒɡbʊk/: Pilots keep a logbook to record all their flight hours and details.
  9. instrument flying /ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈflaɪɪŋ/: She was skilled in instrument flying, which is crucial for flying in bad weather.
  10. feminist /ˈfemənɪst/: She was a feminist icon who challenged traditional gender roles.
  11. celebrity /səˈlebrəti/: Her fame made her a global celebrity.
  12. record-setting /ˈrekɔːd setɪŋ/: She was known for her numerous record-setting flights.
  13. equator /ɪˈkweɪtə(r)/: Her final goal was to fly around the world at the equator.
  14. speculation /ˌspekjuˈleɪʃn/: Her fate has been the subject of endless speculation.
  15. courage /ˈkʌrɪdʒ/: Her courage and determination inspired a generation.
  16. advocate /ˈædvəkət/: She was a strong advocate for women's rights.
  17. legacy /ˈleɡəsi/: Her legacy continues to inspire explorers and aviators today.
  18. ambitious /æmˈbɪʃəs/: Her plan to circumnavigate the globe was her most ambitious project.
  19. aircraft /ˈeəkrɑːft/: She flew a specially modified Lockheed Electra 10E aircraft.
  20. mystery /ˈmɪstri/: The mystery of what happened to her has never been solved.

Warm-up Questions

  1. Amelia Earhart became a symbol of female independence. China has its own historical heroines, like Hua Mulan (花木兰). What qualities do these powerful female figures share?
  2. She famously said, "The most effective way to do it, is to do it." How does this attitude compare to the Chinese idiom "纸上谈兵" (zhǐ shàng tán bīng - discussing military strategy on paper)? Why is taking action more important than just talking?
  3. Her disappearance is a famous mystery. People are fascinated by unsolved mysteries. Can you think of any famous unsolved mysteries or stories from Chinese history or culture?
  4. She was an advocate for women entering new fields like aviation. In China today, what fields are women making the biggest impact in that were once dominated by men?
  5. If you had the chance to be the first person to do something incredible, like explore a new planet, what would motivate you more: the personal achievement or the desire to inspire others?

Reading Passage

Amelia Earhart was a famous pioneer in aviation. She showed great courage and became a global celebrity. She was the first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean. She became an icon of adventure for many people.

Known for her many record-setting flights, which required great skill and endurance, Earhart was also a strong advocate for women's rights and a prominent feminist figure. Her most ambitious plan involved flying her special aircraft around the world near the equator, a journey she carefully documented in her logbook.

Accompanied by her navigator, her attempt at a global circumnavigation, which was a challenging transatlantic flight, ended in tragedy. Her disappearance over the Pacific remains an unsolved mystery, prompting endless speculation. Despite this, her true legacy is her impact on challenging gender roles. Proving her technical mastery in a male-dominated field, her expertise in complex skills like instrument flying left a lasting mark on history.

Ada Lovelace: The First Computer Programmer

Vocabulary

  1. mathematician /ˌmæθəməˈtɪʃn/: She was a brilliant mathematician with a visionary mind.
  2. visionary /ˈvɪʒənri/: Her visionary ideas were centuries ahead of her time.
  3. Analytical Engine /ˌænəˈlɪtɪkl ˈendʒɪn/: She wrote the first algorithm for Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine.
  4. algorithm /ˈælɡərɪðəm/: An algorithm is a set of rules to be followed in calculations.
  5. programmer /ˈprəʊɡræmə(r)/: She is considered the world's first computer programmer.
  6. computation /ˌkɒmpjuˈteɪʃn/: She realized that the machine could go beyond simple computation.
  7. potential /pəˈtenʃl/: She saw the creative potential of computing machines.
  8. collaboration /kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃn/: Her collaboration with Charles Babbage was historically significant.
  9. translation /trænsˈleɪʃn/: Her famous work was published as notes in a translation of an article.
  10. notes /nəʊts/: Her own notes were three times longer than the original article.
  11. poetical science /ˈpəʊətɪkəl ˈsaɪəns/: She described her approach as "poetical science."
  12. symbolic logic /sɪmˌbɒlɪk ˈlɒdʒɪk/: She understood that the machine could manipulate symbols, not just numbers.
  13. legacy /ˈleɡəsi/: Her legacy was not fully appreciated until the modern computer age.
  14. pioneering /ˌpaɪəˈnɪərɪŋ/: Her pioneering work laid the foundation for modern computing.
  15. theoretical /θɪəˈretɪkl/: The Analytical Engine was a theoretical machine that was never fully built.
  16. aristocracy /ˌærɪˈstɒkrəsi/: As the daughter of Lord Byron, she was a member of the British aristocracy.
  17. insight /ˈɪnsaɪt/: Her key insight was that a machine could create music or art.
  18. recognition /ˌrekəɡˈnɪʃn/: She did not receive full recognition for her work during her lifetime.
  19. abstract /ˈæbstrækt/: She was able to grasp the abstract concepts of computing.
  20. concept /ˈkɒnsept/: She introduced the concept of a multi-purpose computing machine.

Warm-up Questions

  1. Ada Lovelace combined imagination ("poetical science") with logic. In your studies, especially for exams like the Gaokao, how important is it to balance creative thinking with logical reasoning?
  2. She saw that computers could create art and music, not just calculate numbers. Today, we have AI that can write poems and create images. Do you think AI can be truly creative? Why or why not?
  3. Her work wasn't fully recognized until 100 years later. Can you think of any figures in Chinese history or science whose contributions were only appreciated long after they lived?
  4. Ada Lovelace is the "mother" of programming. Today, China is a global leader in technology and has many famous tech entrepreneurs. What characteristics do you think are necessary to be an innovator in technology?
  5. She collaborated with Charles Babbage. In science and technology, how important is teamwork and collaboration compared to individual genius?

Reading Passage

Ada Lovelace was a smart mathematician from the English aristocracy. She is now known as the first computer programmer. She had many new ideas. Her work was very important for computers today. Her legacy is very big.

Her pioneering work began through a collaboration with the inventor Charles Babbage on his theoretical machine, the Analytical Engine. While completing a translation of an academic paper about it, she added her own extensive notes. Within these notes, she published the first algorithm and demonstrated that she understood the machine's great potential.

Lovelace's key achievement, however, was her visionary insight into the future of computing. She introduced the concept of "poetical science," which blended imagination with logic. Grasping the abstract principles of symbolic logic, she predicted that a machine could go beyond mere computation to create art and music. Although she received little recognition in her lifetime, her cultural significance as a foundational female figure in STEM is now immense.

Al-Jazari: The Father of Robotics

Vocabulary

  1. polymath /ˈpɒlimæθ/: Al-Jazari was a true polymath: an inventor, engineer, artist, and scholar.
  2. automation /ˌɔːtəˈmeɪʃn/: He is credited with creating some of the first examples of automation.
  3. automata /ɔːˈtɒmətə/: His book describes many fascinating automata, or self-operating machines.
  4. ingenious /ɪnˈdʒiːniəs/: He designed many ingenious mechanical devices.
  5. manuscript /ˈmænjuskrɪpt/: His famous manuscript is called "The Book of Knowledge of Ingenious Mechanical Devices."
  6. camshaft /ˈkæmʃɑːft/: He invented the camshaft, a key component in modern machines.
  7. crankshaft /ˈkræŋkʃɑːft/: The crankshaft was one of his most important inventions, converting rotary to linear motion.
  8. hydro-powered /ˌhaɪdrəʊˈpaʊəd/: Many of his inventions were hydro-powered, using water for energy.
  9. mechanism /ˈmekənɪzəm/: He described over 100 different mechanical mechanisms.
  10. robotics /rəʊˈbɒtɪks/: His work is considered a foundational text in the history of robotics.
  11. scholar /ˈskɒlə(r)/: He was a respected scholar during the Islamic Golden Age.
  12. practical /ˈpræktɪkl/: His inventions were designed for practical purposes, like raising water.
  13. legacy /ˈleɡəsi/: His legacy can be seen in many principles of modern engineering.
  14. craftsmanship /ˈkrɑːftsmənʃɪp/: The illustrations in his book show incredible artistic craftsmanship.
  15. programmable /prəʊˈɡræməbl/: He created a programmable musical automaton, which some call the first robot.
  16. water clock /ˈwɔːtə klɒk/: His most famous creation was a massive, intricate water clock known as the castle clock.
  17. engineering /ˌendʒɪˈnɪərɪŋ/: He made fundamental contributions to the field of mechanical engineering.
  18. innovation /ˌɪnəˈveɪʃn/: His work was a source of great technological innovation.
  19. fountain /ˈfaʊntən/: He designed ornate fountains that changed their patterns automatically.
  20. blueprint /ˈbluːprɪnt/: His book served as a blueprint for future engineers.

Warm-up Questions

  1. Al-Jazari lived during the Islamic Golden Age, a time of great scientific progress. China also had its own "golden ages," like the Tang or Song Dynasty, which produced the Four Great Inventions (四大发明). What conditions do you think are necessary for a society to produce so much innovation?
  2. He created a programmable musical automaton. Today we have robots that can serve food and build cars. What do you think is the future of robotics in China? What jobs might be done by robots in 20 years?
  3. His inventions, like the crankshaft, are still fundamental to modern engines. This shows how ancient ideas can be very powerful. Can you think of an ancient Chinese philosophy or idea (like from Confucius or Laozi) that is still very relevant today?
  4. Al-Jazari was a "polymath" skilled in many areas. In China's education system, students often specialize very early. What are the advantages and disadvantages of being a specialist versus a generalist or polymath?
  5. His castle clock was not just functional but also beautiful. How important is for modern technology (like smartphones or electric cars) to have good design and be aesthetically pleasing?

Reading Passage

Al-Jazari was a famous inventor and scholar from long ago. He was a polymath, which means he was good at many things. He made many ingenious machines. His work was very important for engineering and his legacy is still with us today.

Famous for his detailed manuscript that described over 100 machines and automata, Al-Jazari's work was a great source of innovation and an early step in automation. He designed many practical devices, including hydro-powered machines, a beautiful automated fountain, and a huge water clock, all of which showed amazing craftsmanship.

However, his key achievements in mechanical engineering were inventing the crankshaft and the camshaft, two of the most critical inventions in history. Each mechanism was carefully described, providing a blueprint for future engineers. He even created a programmable musical automaton, which many consider a foundational step in robotics. His work, representing the virtues of creativity and precision, is a prime example of the scientific flourishing during the Islamic Golden Age.

Brian Skerry: The Ocean's Storyteller

Vocabulary

  1. photojournalist /ˌfəʊtəʊˈdʒɜːnəlɪst/: He is a world-renowned underwater photojournalist.
  2. conservation /ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃn/: His work is deeply connected to ocean conservation.
  3. advocacy /ˈædvəkəsi/: He uses his photography as a tool for advocacy.
  4. expedition /ˌekspəˈdɪʃn/: He has spent over 10,000 hours underwater on expeditions.
  5. marine /məˈriːn/: His goal is to reveal the beauty and fragility of marine life.
  6. ecosystem /ˈiːkəʊsɪstəm/: He documents diverse ocean ecosystems from the poles to the tropics.
  7. endangered species /ɪnˌdeɪndʒəd ˈspiːʃiːz/: He has photographed many endangered species to raise awareness.
  8. assignment /əˈsaɪnmənt/: He has completed more than 30 major assignments for National Geographic.
  9. empathy /ˈempəθi/: He believes his photos can create empathy for marine animals.
  10. fragility /frəˈdʒɪləti/: His images often highlight the fragility of ocean environments.
  11. perspective /pəˈspektɪv/: He offers a unique perspective on a world most people never see.
  12. visual storytelling /ˈvɪʒuəl ˈstɔːritelɪŋ/: He is a master of visual storytelling.
  13. illuminate /ɪˈluːmɪneɪt/: His work helps to illuminate the problems facing our oceans.
  14. poaching /ˈpəʊtʃɪŋ/: He has documented the devastating effects of poaching on sea life.
  15. habitat loss /ˈhæbɪtæt lɒs/: His photos show the impact of climate change and habitat loss.
  16. intimate /ˈɪntɪmət/: He captures intimate moments in the lives of sea creatures.
  17. acclaim /əˈkleɪm/: His work has received widespread international acclaim.
  18. stunning /ˈstʌnɪŋ/: He takes stunning photographs that captivate audiences.
  19. awareness /əˈweənəs/: The primary goal of his work is to raise public awareness.
  20. legacy /ˈleɡəsi/: His legacy will be a visual record of our planet's oceans at a critical time.

Warm-up Questions

  1. Brian Skerry uses photos to tell stories and promote conservation. In China, public figures like Yao Ming have used their fame to advocate for causes like shark fin soup reduction. Why are images and famous people so powerful for raising awareness?
  2. He spends thousands of hours in a difficult and dangerous environment to get the perfect photo. What kind of passion and dedication is required for this work? Does it remind you of the dedication needed for your own goals, like preparing for a major exam?
  3. His work creates empathy for animals. The Chinese philosopher Zhuangzi (庄子) once debated if we can know the happiness of fish. Do you think it's possible for humans to truly understand and feel empathy for animals? How?
  4. Skerry documents the impact of problems like pollution. China is making great efforts to tackle environmental issues. What are some of the biggest environmental challenges and successes you have seen in your own city or region?
  5. If you could be a photojournalist for a day, what story would you want to tell with your pictures?

Reading Passage

Brian Skerry is an underwater photojournalist. He takes stunning pictures of marine life. He goes on a long expedition for each assignment. His work has received a lot of acclaim. His photos help with ocean conservation.

A master of visual storytelling, Skerry uses his camera for advocacy to help protect the oceans. He takes intimate pictures that offer a unique perspective on sea creatures. His main goal is to raise public awareness about the problems that face the ocean ecosystem and its many endangered species.

Ultimately, his work embodies the virtues of patience and dedication, using his talent to illuminate the ocean's profound fragility. Documenting the tragic effects of poaching and habitat loss, his photographs create deep empathy in viewers. In an era of environmental crisis, his work is critically relevant. Therefore, his legacy will be a powerful visual record that inspires a global audience to become stewards of the blue heart of our world.

Ernest Shackleton: The Leader Who Never Gave Up

Vocabulary

  1. explorer /ɪkˈsplɔːrə(r)/: He was a polar explorer who led three British expeditions to the Antarctic.
  2. expedition /ˌekspəˈdɪʃn/: His most famous expedition was the Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition.
  3. endurance /ɪnˈdjʊərəns/: The ship was named "Endurance," which became a symbol of their ordeal.
  4. leadership /ˈliːdəʃɪp/: His leadership is studied as a model of how to manage a crisis.
  5. survival /səˈvaɪvl/: The story is one of the greatest survival tales of all time.
  6. perilous /ˈperələs/: They undertook a perilous 800-mile journey in a small boat.
  7. marooned /məˈruːnd/: The crew was marooned on the ice for months.
  8. morale /məˈrɑːl/: Shackleton worked constantly to keep his crew's morale high.
  9. optimism /ˈɒptɪmɪzəm/: His unwavering optimism was crucial to their survival.
  10. resourcefulness /rɪˈsɔːsflnəs/: The crew showed incredible resourcefulness in adapting to their situation.
  11. ordeal /ɔːˈdiːl/: Despite the two-year ordeal, not a single member of his crew was lost.
  12. Antarctic /ænˈtɑːktɪk/: The Antarctic environment is the harshest on Earth.
  13. ice floe /ˈaɪs fləʊ/: They lived on a drifting ice floe after their ship sank.
  14. legendary /ˈledʒəndri/: His journey to get help has become legendary.
  15. adversity /ədˈvɜːsəti/: He showed incredible courage in the face of extreme adversity.
  16. objective /əbˈdʒektɪv/: When the ship was lost, his objective changed from exploration to rescue.
  17. loyalty /ˈlɔɪəlti/: His crew showed immense loyalty to him throughout the ordeal.
  18. determination /dɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃn/: His determination to save his men never weakened.
  19. navigate /ˈnævɪɡeɪt/: They had to navigate some of the most dangerous seas in the world.
  20. legacy /ˈleɡəsi/: His legacy is one of ultimate leadership and human endurance.

Warm-up Questions

  1. Shackleton's main goal failed, but he is remembered as a hero. This is similar to the Chinese saying "失败是成功之母" (shībài shì chénggōng zhī mǔ - failure is the mother of success). Why can failure sometimes be more valuable than success?
  2. His greatest skill was maintaining his team's morale and optimism in a desperate situation. In a team, like a sports team or a study group, why is the leader's attitude so important for the group's success?
  3. The story of the Endurance is a famous tale of survival against nature. Can you think of any Chinese stories or historical events where people showed incredible determination to survive a natural disaster?
  4. Shackleton's objective changed from exploration to saving his men. Good leaders must be able to adapt. Describe a situation where you had to change your plan because of unexpected problems.
  5. The crew had absolute loyalty to Shackleton. What qualities do you think inspire such strong loyalty in a leader?

Reading Passage
